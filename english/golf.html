Golf Course

The Fairway

The main mowed area of grass between the tee and the green. Players aim to hit their shots here for better accuracy.

The Green

The final putting area surrounding the hole. This is the shortest grass and where the most precision is required.

The Rough

Longer grass on the sides of the fairway. Hitting into the rough makes the next shot more difficult.

Game Rules

Essential rules every golf fan should know

  • The objective is to play the ball from the tee into the hole in the fewest strokes possible.
  • A standard golf course has 18 holes, though 9-hole courses also exist.
  • Each hole has a par score (usually 3, 4, or 5) indicating the expected number of strokes.
  • A birdie is one stroke under par; an eagle is two strokes under par.
  • A bogey is one stroke over par; a double bogey is two strokes over par.
  • Players must play the ball as it lies, except in specific permitted situations.
  • In match play, the player with the lowest score on a hole wins that hole.
  • In stroke play, the player with the lowest total score wins the tournament.
  • Lost balls result in a one-stroke penalty, and the player plays a new ball.
  • Out of bounds shots result in a one-stroke penalty, and the player replays from the original position.
