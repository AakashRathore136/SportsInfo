Cricket Ground

The Pitch

A rectangular 22-yard strip in the center where most action occurs. Made of clay and grass.

The Boundary

The outer edge of the field, marked by a rope. Hitting the ball over it scores 4 or 6 runs.

The Crease

White lines at each end of the pitch marking safe zones for batsmen.

Game Rules

Essential rules every cricket fan should know

  • Each team has 11 players. One team bats while the other bowls and fields.
  • The batting team tries to score runs by hitting the ball and running between wickets.
  • A run is scored when both batsmen successfully reach the opposite crease.
  • Hitting the ball to the boundary rope scores 4 runs; over the boundary scores 6 runs.
  • The bowling team tries to dismiss batsmen through various methods: bowled, caught, LBW, run out, stumped.
  • An over consists of 6 legal deliveries bowled by one bowler.
  • Test matches last up to 5 days; ODIs have 50 overs per side; T20s have 20 overs per side.
  • The team with the most runs wins. If all batsmen are dismissed, the innings ends.
  • A no-ball (overstepping) or wide (outside reach) gives the batting team an extra run.
  • The umpire's decision is final, though DRS (Decision Review System) allows limited reviews.
